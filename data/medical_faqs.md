# Medical FAQs Knowledge Base

## Snake Bites
- **Q**: What should I do if someone is bitten by a snake?
- **A**: Keep the person calm and still. Immobilize the bitten limb and keep it at or below heart level. Seek immediate medical attention. Do NOT cut the wound, do NOT try to suck out the venom, and do NOT apply a tourniquet.

## Fever
- **Q**: How to manage a high fever at home?
- **A**: Drink plenty of fluids (water, ORS, soups). Rest as much as possible. Use a damp cloth on the forehead for cooling. If the fever exceeds 103°F (39.4°C) or lasts more than 3 days, consult a doctor.

## First Aid for Wounds
- **Q**: How to treat a minor cut or wound?
- **A**: Wash your hands first. Apply gentle pressure with a clean cloth to stop bleeding. Clean the wound with clear water. Apply a thin layer of antibiotic ointment and cover with a bandage.

## Dehydration
- **Q**: What are the signs of dehydration?
- **A**: Extreme thirst, less frequent urination, dark-colored urine, fatigue, dizziness, and confusion. In rural areas, use Oral Rehydration Salts (ORS) or a mix of salt, sugar, and clean water.

## Chest Pain
- **Q**: Is chest pain always a heart attack?
- **A**: Not always, but it should ALWAYS be treated as an emergency. If you experience chest pain, shortness of breath, or pain radiating to the jaw or left arm, call 108 or go to the hospital immediately.
